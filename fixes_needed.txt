# Korean Study — Design Audit
*June 2026 · iPhone-first Korean habit app · goal: best-in-class path to C1 reading*

---

## North star

> Build the iPhone app that makes a daily Korean habit feel inevitable — and
> makes the multi-year climb to C1 reading feel visible, achievable, and worth
> celebrating every single day.

Every change below serves one of two pillars:

1. **Korean-native soul** — Hangul is the product, and the design should feel
   like it was built *for* Korean, not translated from a generic flashcard tool.
2. **Emotional progression arc** — streaks measure yesterday; the design must
   also make the destination (C1) feel real and close enough to pull learners
   forward across years.

---

## Priority key

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **P0** | Foundational — do first; blocks everything else feeling right |
| **P1** | High-impact — significant retention / delight uplift |
| **P2** | Polish & delight — separates good from best-in-class |

## 1 · Korean typography `P0`

The single biggest gap. The app loads only Geist and lets the OS render Hangul —
so 한국어 looks different on every device, never pairs intentionally with the Latin
UI, and is visually demoted to a system fallback. For a Korean app this is like a
cookbook with no food photos.

### Recommended changes

- **Adopt Pretendard** (or Apple SD Gothic Neo as the iOS-native fallback) as a
  dedicated `--font-korean` token. Pretendard is purpose-built to pair with
  Inter/Geist-class Latin sans, has every weight (100–900), and gives Hangul an
  even, modern color across platforms.
- **Distinct type treatment for Hangul vs. gloss.** Korean needs ~1.6–1.7 leading
  and slightly more letter-spacing than Latin at the same px size. English glosses
  should be smaller and lighter to visually subordinate to the Korean they explain.
  Today both share one type ramp.
- **Flashcard Hangul scale.** 40 px is the floor for the front-of-card hero word;
  48–56 px with real optical tracking is ideal. The card is the most-seen surface
  in the app — it should feel generous, not cramped.
- **Add a reading-size control and respect Dynamic Type.** Learners reading towards
  C1 encounter long, complex sentences. They must be able to scale text without
  breaking the layout.

## 2 · Color & brand identity `P1`

### What's working
Clean, calm palette. The single blue-500 accent keeps things from getting busy.

### Problems

- **The accent collides with the taxonomy.** Primary CTA is blue-500; "vocabulary"
  badges are *also* blue. The color doing "main action" should not also mean a
  content category — it creates ambiguity in both directions.
- **No warm color for reward.** The palette is entirely cool/clinical. Habit
  products need a warm "you did it" color that the streak and goal-completion
  system owns. A coral or amber secondary would carry celebration without
  compromising the clean everyday feel.
- **The yellow highlight is under-designed.** `yellow-200` on white is
  low-contrast and reads like a browser default highlighter. This is the
  signature element of the app — it deserves a bespoke token: slightly deeper
  fill, AA-contrast text, soft rounded "marker" feel.
- **Dark mode is unrefined.** `#0b0f1a` (very black-navy) with a single surface
  level reads as an inversion, not a designed experience. Evening study is when
  dark mode is used most — it deserves first-class surface tiers (base, elevated,
  modal) and its own light/shadow system.

### Recommended changes

- Recolor card-type taxonomy off the primary: vocabulary → indigo, grammar →
  violet, phrase → teal. Reserve blue strictly for *action*.
- Add one warm secondary token (`--reward`) for streaks, goal rings, and
  celebration moments.
- Promote the sentence highlight to a design token (`--highlight-bg`,
  `--highlight-fg`) with a crafted, AA-compliant value.
- Build dark mode as a three-tier surface system (`--surface-1/2/3`) rather than
  a single background color.

## 3 · The sentence highlight — push the signature element further `P1`

The highlighted sentence is the app's most distinctive mechanic. Right now it is
a single `<mark>` tag. It should be the richest object in the product.

### Recommended changes

- **Tap-to-gloss.** Tap any word in the sentence → inline definition / dictionary
  form popover. Turns every sentence into a mini-reader. Directly serves the C1
  reading goal.
- **Audio.** A speaker affordance on the sentence and target form (TTS now, native
  voice actor later). Reading + listening together is how reading fluency builds;
  it also supports learners studying away from a desk.
- **Pitch/structure cues for grammar cards.** Highlight the particle vs. the stem
  in different tints — a grammar card already knows the `targetForm`; use that
  structure visually.
- **Reading-aid toggle.** Optional spacing or segmentation helper for beginners,
  off by default for intermediate+ learners. Respects the range from A2 to C1
  without cluttering the default experience.

## 4 · The study loop `P0`

This is where 90% of learner time is spent. Every rough edge here compounds.

### Problems

- **The grading bar is Anki-UI.** Again / Hard / Good / Easy as four equal
  candy-colored pills is power-user vocabulary that intimidates beginners and isn't
  iOS-native. It is the right *information* (FSRS needs 4 signals) but the wrong
  *presentation*.
- **The card answer has no physical feel.** The answer fades up in 150 ms. A real
  3D flip (or a crisp spring-driven slide) makes the core interaction feel like a
  polished app, not a webpage.
- **The schedule payoff is invisible.** Interval hints (`3d`, `7d`) exist but are
  tiny and abstract. Beginners have no model of what "3 days" means in terms of
  mastery.
- **Progress is presented as a stat, not a shape.** `1 reviewed · 19 left` is
  fine data; it is not satisfying to watch fill up.

### Recommended changes

- **Replace or redesign the grading bar.** Option A: swipe gestures — swipe left
  = Again, swipe right = Got it; the 4-grade nuance appears on long-press for
  advanced users. Option B: two primary buttons (Got it / Missed it) with Hard/Easy
  as a secondary refinement. Pair every grade with haptic feedback.
- **Card flip.** Add a real 3D CSS flip transform (180° Y-axis, spring easing) on
  reveal. This is one animation, and it defines the entire kinesthetic feel of the
  app.
- **Frame intervals as mastery language.** *"You'll see this again in 3 days"* →
  *"Memory strengthening to 3 days"* → *"Long-term memory — next review in 3
  weeks."* Celebrate growing intervals as progress, not scheduling metadata.
- **Progress ring.** Replace or supplement the top progress bar with a ring that
  fills arc-by-arc so the session has a satisfying visual shape.
- **Session-complete moment.** Make it a real emotional beat: confetti burst, streak
  update, today's goal ring closing. Right now it is three small stat tiles and a
  "Study again" button.

## 5 · Habit & progression system `P0`

The retention engine. The most important screens in the app after the study loop.

### Problems

- **Streaks are brittle.** One missed day wipes the streak. This is the #1 churn
  moment in every habit app ever shipped. There is no forgiveness, no freeze, no
  "rest day" concept.
- **No milestone celebration.** 7 days, 30 days, 100 days — nothing happens. The
  🔥 number increments silently.
- **The goal bar is functional, not emotional.** A horizontal `h-2.5` bar does the
  job. A ring that *closes* is one of the  most effective habit mechanics ever
  shipped, and it is iOS-native vocabulary.

### Recommended changes

- **Streak forgiveness.** Add streak freezes / grace days (one per week earned,
  or purchasable). Frame rest days as part of the habit, not a failure of it. Add
  an at-risk nudge: *"Keep your 24-day streak — 5 min left today."*
- **Milestone moments.** At 7 / 30 / 100 / 365 days, surface a special
  full-screen celebration with a personal stat summary.
- **Habit ring.** Replace or supplement the linear goal bar with a circular
  ring — the ring closing is the primary daily reward trigger.
- **Heatmap insight line.** The heatmap already tells a data story; add one
  generated sentence beneath it: *"Your best streak day is Tuesday."* *"You've
  studied 142 days this year."* Data without narrative is just squares.

## 6 · Home dashboard `P1`

### Problems

- **Three equal stat tiles give equal weight to unequal things.** "Due today" is
  the only number that drives action. "Total Cards" and "Lessons" are reference
  numbers. They should not compete with each other visually.
- **The Sync panel is back-office plumbing on the home screen.** It belongs in
  Settings or behind a pull-to-refresh gesture, not as a primary card.
- **The "Study now" CTA is present but not obviously primary.** It is the same
  visual weight as the habit tracker card.

### Recommended changes

- **Hero: due count + study CTA.** One large, warm, prominent block for "X cards
  ready — Study now." Everything else recedes.
- **Secondary row.** Total cards / lessons / level as a quiet three-column stats
  strip below, smaller and lighter.
- **Move Sync to Settings.** The home screen should be entirely motivational, not
  administrative.
- **Context greeting.** A time-aware one-liner: *"Good evening — 12 cards to lock
  in before midnight."* The data is already there (day-start hour, due count);
  the voice just needs to surface it.

## 7 · Cards / content management `P2`

### Problems

- The Cards screen stacks search + 4 filter pills + lesson-range selects + group
  toggles before any content is visible — high cognitive load before the learner
  has a reason to filter anything.
- The inline blue `CardEditor` form is not iOS-native; it opens in-place and has
  no clear dismiss gesture.

### Recommended changes

- **Sticky search/filter bar.** Collapse all filters into a single bar with a
  filter icon that opens a bottom sheet — content breathes, and the common case
  (browse all cards) is zero-friction.
- **iOS-native rows.** Swipe-to-delete on card rows; the edit form slides up as
  a modal sheet rather than expanding in-place.
- **Promote the Sentences view.** This is the bridge to C1 reading — it surfaces
  every example sentence the learner has encountered. Label it prominently: *"Reading
  practice"* rather than the hidden tab label "sentences."

## 8 · Navigation & information architecture `P1`

Five tabs is one too many for a focused, single-goal app.

### Recommended changes

- **Remove Settings from the tab bar.** Move it behind an avatar/profile icon in
  the top-right corner of the nav bar — this is the iOS convention for settings in
  a focused-mode app, and it frees a tab slot.
- **Consider merging Habits into Home** as the hero section, freeing the tab bar
  for a future **Read** tab (graded reading passages — the literal C1 destination).
  The proposed five-tab end state: Home / Study / Read / Cards / Profile.
- **Active tab haptics.** The 2.5-stroke icon treatment is correct; add a haptic
  on switch to complete the iOS-native feel.

## 9 · iOS-native craft & motion `P1`

The biggest gap between "web app in a phone frame" and "feels like it was built
for iPhone."

### Recommended changes

- **iOS layout conventions.** Large navigation titles (34 pt), grouped inset card
  lists, bottom sheets for transient tasks (CardEditor, lesson filter, mode select),
  system blur (`backdrop-filter: blur(20px) saturate(180%)`) for sticky bars.
- **Motion system.** Define three animation primitives:
  - *Spring* — card flip, sheet present/dismiss, progress ring.
  - *Ease-out* — progress bar fills, list items entering.
  - *Burst* — goal met, streak milestone, session complete.
  Today there is exactly one animation (150 ms fade). One deliberate motion system
  at three levels is all that is needed.
- **Haptics.** Grade tap (selection), correct answer (success notification), goal
  ring close (impact heavy), streak milestone (impact medium). Free, and
  transformative on device.
- **Pull-to-refresh.** On the home screen to sync. Removes the need for a Sync
  card entirely.

## 10 · Accessibility & inclusivity `P1`

A reading-to-C1 app should be fully operable one-handed, on a commute, by everyone.

### Recommended changes

- **Contrast audit.** The `yellow-200` highlight, `gray-400` hint text, and several
  badge foreground/background pairs are likely below WCAG AA (4.5:1 for text,
  3:1 for UI components). Fix at the token level.
- **44 pt minimum tap targets everywhere.** Filter pills, sentence remove buttons
  (✕), grade interval hints, and small inline actions are currently below this.
  Enforce `min-height: 44px; min-width: 44px` as a utility.
- **VoiceOver labels.** The heatmap cells, streak dots, progress ring, and grade
  buttons all need descriptive `aria-label` values. The study loop must be fully
  operable without touch (keyboard, Switch Control).
- **Reduce motion.** The card flip and celebration burst should respect
  `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## 11 · Voice, delight & the long game `P2`

### What's working
The copy voice is already a strength — calm, encouraging, sentence-case, no hype.
The 🔥 / ✓ emoji discipline is correct.

### Recommended additions

- **Use the copy voice at emotional beats it currently misses.** Session complete,
  streak milestone, band unlock, comeback after a missed day, hitting 100 days of
  study. The warmth is there in the codebase; it just needs more moments to speak.
- **A real app icon and a small identity mark.** The current icon is a placeholder
  blue disc. A real mark — even a simple stylized 한 or a meaningful geometric —
  gives the habit something to feel loyal to. Keep it restrained; the goal is
  recognition, not a mascot.
- **Long-game identity: "My Korean" summary.** A shareable yearly / milestone stat
  card (à la Spotify Wrapped, but quarterly). *"In 90 days you reviewed 1,240 cards,
  built a 24-day streak, and mastered Lessons 7–12."* Both a reward and organic
  sharing surface.

## Suggested implementation sequence

### Phase 1 — Foundations `P0` (do first)
1. Korean typeface — add Pretendard; update `--font-korean`; re-scale Hangul in cards.
2. Study loop — 3D card flip; redesign grading bar (swipe or 2-tier); session-complete moment.
3. Proficiency ladder — A1→C1 visible arc in the dashboard and habits views.

### Phase 2 — Identity & retention `P1`
4. Color system — recolor taxonomy off primary; add warm `--reward` token; refine highlight token; rebuild dark-mode surface tiers.
5. Habit ring — circular daily-goal ring; streak forgiveness; milestone celebrations.
6. Dashboard hierarchy — hero CTA; move sync to Settings; context greeting.
7. Navigation — remove Settings tab; add profile icon; plan Read tab slot.
8. iOS conventions — bottom sheets, pull-to-refresh, motion system, haptics.
9. Accessibility — contrast pass, 44pt targets, VoiceOver labels, reduce-motion.

### Phase 3 — Polish `P2`
10. Tap-to-gloss + audio on sentences.
12. Sentence highlight as a fully crafted design token.
13. App icon and brand mark.
