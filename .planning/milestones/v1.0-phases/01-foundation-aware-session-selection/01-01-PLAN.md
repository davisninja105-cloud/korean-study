<objective>
Make study-session *selection* prerequisite-coherent. Today `GET /api/cards/due` caps to the
most-due `sessionSize` cards BEFORE sequencing and fetches prerequisite edges only among those
already-capped IDs — so a due dependent can enter a session while its (also-due) prerequisite is
bumped out, and the size cap is never accounted for during selection.

This plan adds a pure `selectSessionCards` selector (alongside `sequenceCards` in `lib/sequence.ts`)
that operates over the whole eligible pool, pulls each chosen card's still-due in-pool prerequisites
into the session (downward-closed under the prerequisite relation), and applies the `sessionSize` cap
*during* selection. The existing `sequenceCards()` reordering stays in place and runs after selection.

Purpose: Honor the app's "foundation-first" promise at the selection layer, not just as a reshuffle
of a possibly-incoherent set (the milestone Core Value: what you're meant to learn is always learnable
in the moment — prerequisites come first).

Output: One new exported pure function, its unit tests, and a surgical rewire of the due-cards route.

## Phase Goal

**As a** Korean learner studying due cards, **I want to** never be quizzed on a word whose
still-unlearned prerequisites got bumped out of the session by the size cap, **so that** every card
in my session is actually learnable in the moment — its foundations are present and shown first.

The vertical slice for this phase is the due-cards selection path end-to-end: pool `findMany`
(`take: 1000`) → `selectSessionCards` → `sequenceCards` → JSON response, plus its unit tests.
There is NO UI in this phase. This is Part 1 ONLY of `foundation-first-plan.md` — no `lib/known-words.ts`,
no `countUnknownWords`, no `unknownCount` annotation, no `components/StudySession.tsx` change (all Phase 2).
