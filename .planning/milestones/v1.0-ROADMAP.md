# Roadmap: Korean Study — Foundation-First Study

## Overview

Two phases that together make the "foundation-first" promise real. Phase 1 fixes session selection so a due dependent never enters a session without its still-unlearned prerequisites. Phase 2 fixes presentation so new words are shown bare before context, and sentences shown to the learner prefer words already known. The milestone is complete when a real study session feels coherent: foundations always come first, and new words are learnable in the moment.

## Phase Details

### Phase 1: Foundation-Aware Session Selection

**Goal**: Sessions are prerequisite-coherent — a due dependent never appears without its still-unlearned, in-pool prerequisites, and the session size cap operates during selection rather than after it
**Mode:** mvp
**Depends on**: Nothing (first phase)
**Requirements**: SEL-01, SEL-02, SEL-03, SEL-04, SEL-05, QUAL-01
**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE):

  1. A due dependent card whose prerequisite is also due (but less overdue) now appears in the session alongside that prerequisite, with the prerequisite sequenced first
  2. The session never contains a card whose in-pool prerequisite was bumped out by the size cap — the selected set is downward-closed under the prerequisite relation
  3. Session size overshoot is bounded to at most one prerequisite closure beyond `sessionSize`
  4. Dependency cycles (if any exist in the data) do not cause infinite recursion or duplicate cards
  5. `selectSessionCards` unit tests pass: pool ≤ size returns all; due dependent pulls in less-due prereq; output is downward-closed and capped; cycle-safe; overshoot bounded

**Plans**: 1/1 plans complete

### Phase 2: Maturity- & Known-Word-Aware Presentation

**Goal**: New cards are introduced as a bare word on the flashcard front; whenever a sentence is shown it is the one with the fewest unknown words; lint and build stay clean; behavior confirmed in a real study session
**Mode:** mvp
**Depends on**: Phase 1
**Requirements**: PRES-01, PRES-02, PRES-03, PRES-04, PRES-05, QUAL-02, QUAL-03, QUAL-04
**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE):

  1. A brand-new card (no review row, or FSRS state New/Learning) shows the bare Korean word on the flashcard front in Exposure mode — no sentence on the front
  2. Flipping that new card reveals the English meaning plus an example sentence as context (with a "recall the meaning" prompt under the bare front before the flip)
  3. When multiple example sentences exist, the one displayed has fewer unknown words than the alternatives; ties still break by the existing rotation
  4. Matured cards continue to show a sentence on the front; multiple-choice continues to show the bare word — no regressions
  5. `npm run lint` and `npm run build` are clean; a manual dev-session walkthrough confirms the bare-word-first and least-unknown-sentence behaviors together
